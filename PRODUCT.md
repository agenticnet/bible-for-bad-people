# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Spiritually exhausted, irreverent, and curious adults — people who grew up around religion (or its absence), feel allergic to piety, but still find scripture culturally fascinating. They arrive skeptical, often amused, sometimes genuinely searching for language to name hypocrisy, guilt, or absurdity. Context: late-night scrolling, group-chat sharing, solo venting after a bad week.

## Product Purpose

Bible for Bad People is a provocative AI-driven sanctuary: nine “chambers” for venting, satire, and cultural autopsy of scripture without pretending to be a church. Success looks like someone laughing, feeling seen, and sharing a passage or chat response — without the product ever feeling like a novelty gift shop Bible or a generic neon sin-app.

## Brand Personality

**Literate. Irreverent. Earnest underneath.**

The voice is sharp and satirical, but the container should feel like something you could read for an hour — a study Bible’s seriousness carrying content your Sunday school skipped. Not preachy; not juvenile.

## Anti-references

- Souvenir-shop Bible aesthetics: gold foil, faux parchment textures, gothic clip art, oversized crosses, “holy glow” filters
- Neon cyberpunk “sin casino” UI (current direction) — purple/cyan glow orbs, gradient borders, scanlines
- Generic AI landing page grammar: eyebrow on every section, identical icon cards, hero metrics
- Tacky religious kitsch: flames, halos, stained-glass gradients used decoratively

## Design Principles

1. **Scripture earns the serif** — long-form and verse content reads like a printed page; UI chrome stays sans.
2. **Satire lives in copy, not chrome** — the frame is dignified; the jokes are in the words.
3. **Restraint over spectacle** — one accent (wine/burgundy), ink-on-paper contrast, no glow.
4. **Anthropic-style type roles** — serif for editorial/display, sans for navigation and controls, mono for references and metadata.
5. **Practice what we preach** — if we quote the Bible, typography should respect the act of reading.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum contrast on all body text and interactive states. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Satirical content warnings where passages are violent or sexual. No essential information conveyed by color alone.
