Bible aesthetic directions

Goal: feel like reading scripture, not a souvenir shop or a neon sin-casino. Typography follows Anthropic’s role split (serif editorial, sans UI, mono metadata) with open-source substitutes.

Survivors

#1

Study Bible (light page, dark binding)

Parchment body, leather-toned header/footer, wine accent. Source Serif 4 + Figtree + JetBrains Mono. Passages as ruled columns, not glowing cards.

  • Why it wins: Dignified frame lets satire live in copy; matches “read the Bible” muscle memory.
  • Anthropic parallel: Ivory editorial surfaces with dark structural chrome; serif headlines, sans controls.

#2

Red-letter reader

Same light page, but ember accent reserved for quoted or divine-voice lines only. Everything else stays ink-neutral.

  • Tradeoff: Powerful when sparse; becomes tacky if overused on buttons and badges.

#3

Full leather dark mode

Entire app on binding-dark with ivory type, like reading a gilt-edged Bible under lamplight.

  • Tradeoff: Atmospheric but harder WCAG on muted text; fights Anthropic’s light editorial default.

Rejected

Faux parchment texture pack

Noise overlays, yellowed paper JPGs, gothic borders. Reads souvenir-shop immediately.

Neon cyberpurgatory (status quo)

Purple/cyan glow, gradient borders, scanlines. Opposite of scripture gravitas.

Church bulletin clipart

Halos, flames, stained-glass gradients as UI chrome. Kitsch without irony control.
